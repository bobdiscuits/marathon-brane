PERIMETER REGION: HAULER

\=================================================================== 

  

LOCATION: West-Central

  

ROLE: The HAULER is the mobile command center for the DATA WALL.

  

OVERVIEW: HAULERs of this type are used to deploy and support DATA WALL placements. Their systems serve as the day-to-day, minute-to-minute point of command for all DATA WALL operations. Typically, a HAULER would work in step with a central command facility. In PERIMETER's case: STATION.

  

The HAULER's onboard monitoring stations, when fully operational, would provide real-time analysis of figures provided by both the DATA WALL as well as any specific research/investigations it may oversee into specific materials, minerals, or other geological points of interest identified by the data it collects.

  

The HAULER's position, dictated by the placement of the DATA WALL, also suggests it was placed using aerial support. PERIMETER's rocky terrain would make standard traversal impossible, indicating that the importance of the work being done here required inconvenient strategic deployments of the equipment on hand.

PERIMETER REGION: SOUTH RELAY

\=================================================================== 

  

LOCATION: Southwest Edge

  

ROLE: A secondary support RELAY built once it was realized that planetary readings collected by the DATA WALL were too dense for a single RELAY to filter. 

  

OVERVIEW: SOUTH RELAY is an incomplete twin. It appears to have been constructed more efficiently than NORTH RELAY, but was never finished. Slightly elevated levels of decay hint at the southern reach of PERIMETER either being less maintained in the colony's waning days, or at some as yet unregistered side effect of the readings they were attempting to capture here.
