# NuCaloric

> Philanthropically branded consumer megacorp that supplied the UESC Marathon's food and civilian goods, operates the colony's agricultural AI **Darius** and the **Dire Marsh** research hub, and holds contractual salvage rights over Tau Ceti IV's colonial goods — with a contested interest in NuCal-flagged **Cerberus** kill orders and active field intelligence via hired Runners.

## What the source establishes — canon

**Corporate identity and classification.** NuCaloric Agricultural (abbreviated NuCal) is classified as a **philanthropically minded consumer megacorp**[^1]. The company's Runners' Guide entry lists its faction agent as **Gaius**[^1].

**Founding and famine response.** NuCaloric came to prominence through its humanitarian response to **the worst famine in recorded history**, which struck Earth and Mars in the mid-2360s when a fungus attacked wheat, rice, sorghum, and other basic grains worldwide[^7]. The company's founder, **Gus Moraine**, aggressively centered public philanthropy as a pillar of NuCaloric's corporate strategy[^1]. NuCaloric's Agriculture division "worked tirelessly to reform how food was grown, stored, and distributed" in the famine's wake — a history subsequently taught in New Cascadia's school curriculum[^7].

**Growth into megacorp.** This mix of social utility and ruthless optimization transformed NuCaloric into the **dominant provider of basic consumer goods in Sol**, diversifying beyond agriculture to everything from hab units to cosmetics[^1].

**Role in the Marathon mission.** NuCaloric signed an **exclusive mission supply contract** with the UESC to supply civilian goods for the UESC Marathon, making them a **legal stakeholder in the colony venture**[^1][^11]. The contract's full scope encompasses attachments "A" through "OR55"[^11]. A UESC draft welcome document names NuCaloric Agricultural alongside Traxus and CyberAcme as the mission's three principal corporate partners[^22]. NuCaloric "delivered the survival basics that the Marathon and its colonists would need for success"[^8].

**Influence over site selection.** During pre-expedition planning, a **NuCaloric Agriculture panel** assessed candidate worlds. The panel rejected Enceladus as impractical for agriculture; NuCaloric agronomists co-authored (with UESC physicists) the approval of **Tau Ceti IV**, asserting that "a more compatible target is unlikely to exist"[^12]. This gave NuCaloric material influence over the colony destination.

**Corporate governance on the Marathon.** Captain di Roxas's colonization protocol lists **David Manaea** as the corporate representative Counselor (Alderman) for NuCal on the colony's governing council, in the Rural/Ag-Hub seat[^5].

**The Seed Vault and agricultural AI Darius.** Colonization protocol assigns **Darius** — one of the eight Rhea-suite colony AIs — to agricultural hub roles, transitioning between AgHub sites as the colony grows[^5]. Dire Marsh is described as "a key ZONE in the colony's sustainability efforts," designed as a **collaboration between Colony Science Command and NuCaloric Agricultural**, focused on "the research and active development of hybrid food sources" — crossbreeding Earth-based nutriment with Tau Ceti-local plants and proteins[^9].

**Novel Contagion and the quarantine.** The Dire Marsh zone entry notes "evidence of a quarantine operation" pointing to "a credible threat to colony security," framing food contamination as an existential risk for a colony removed from its natural habitat[^9]. The NuCal-sponsored xenobiology researcher **Dr. Tobias Luttero** (entomologist, approved by both NuCaloric and UESC security vetting) was slated to work on colony biological research[^6].

**Cerberus code fragments.** NuCaloric recovered code fragments from a damaged UESC unit's command module, annotated by ONI, containing orders from an AI designated **"Cerberus."** The logic tree distinguishes between two NuCal-relevant cases: if `query: contagion = true` (post-2787), execute data harvest + purge and implant CHORDODES cyberwarfare; if `cID: NuCal = true` (post-2787), execute data aggregate + purge and implant TRIOXYS, a parasite compromising WEAVEworm integrity[^2]. The fragment's SUMMARY describes Cerberus as "previously unidentified" and its orders as targeting "hostile ground operations on Tau Ceti IV." NuCaloric holds these fragments under the `factions/contracts` Codex category[^2].

**Salvage and property rights.** Contract clause 616 establishes that all physical goods manufactured by NuCal for exclusive colonial use remain **NuCal's sovereign property, leased to colonists**, with a minimum lease duration of 600 years, automatically renewable in perpetuity, cancellable only by personal legal renunciation from **"NuCal Entity G"**[^11]. Clause 298 specifies that no independent colonial rights or political authority are recognized by either UESC or NuCal[^11]. The Runners' Guide notes that NuCal's current **attempts to enforce salvage rights on Tau Ceti IV are limited in scope**, and the faction "does not appear interested in sustained confrontation with the UESC or other corporate stakeholders, preferring to gather historical data and specific intel using hired Runner operatives"[^1].

**Product line and food synthesis.** NuCaloric's primary consumer product is **NuCaloric 187, the "Drinkable Cheeseburger"** — a shelf-stable liquid food introduced pre-expedition[^14]. Gus Moraine personally recorded a farewell message to Marathon colonists introducing it as a "small gift"[^14]. Internal NuCal board recordings reveal deliberate compliance gaming: NuCal marketing and legal debated disclaimers for NuCaloric 187 specifically to avoid regulatory action before the UESC joint venture, settling on minimalist boilerplate[^19]. On Tau Ceti IV, synthesis technicians adapted NuCal food production equipment to local organic conditions in ways that deviated from official protocols[^18].

**Anti-Virus Pack and Cardio Kick.** NuCaloric Agricultural manufactures the **Anti-Virus Protection Pack**, which installs software integrity protections against data corruption fields[^3]. The **NuCaloric Wellness** sub-brand manufactures **Cardio Kick**, a performance-enhancing nanocoolant injector for Runner shells that boosts agility, stamina, and heat mitigation[^4].

**Debt and the Runner pipeline.** The ONI acceptance notice for an unnamed Runner applicant lists **NuCaloric Agricultural Nourishment Assistance Division** as one of the pre-existing creditors whose debt CyberAcme purchased and added to the Runner's balance[^10]. NuCaloric's debt instruments are thus part of the Runner acquisition pipeline.

**UESC HQ catering.** NuCaloric Culinary Catering operates the cafeteria at UESC Headquarters, providing a full menu of products under the NuCaloric numbered food system (NuCaloric 12, 27, 42, 58, 73, 89, 95, 101, 110, 123)[^20].

**Post-colony presence.** Post-colony survivor notes describe subsisting solely on crates of NuCal shelf-stable packages — the Drinkable Cheeseburger — as the only remaining Earth-origin food[^17]. The notes also mention "NuCal Non-Vanilla Pudding" as an approved colony potluck dish, while the original Drinkable Cheeseburger in packaging was rejected as insufficiently communal[^16]. Dr. Val Cole, during her Anomaly expedition, names a recording device using a NuCaloric product number before switching to a human name[^13].

**Sekiguchi Genetics.** The pack does not contain a direct NuCal-Sekiguchi relationship. Source-silent on any formal affiliation.

## Source-silent / open questions

- **"NuCal Entity G"**: The contract names this entity as the sole authority capable of cancelling the 600-year colonial goods lease. The pack does not identify who or what Entity G is. Source-silent.
- **Agent Gaius**: The Runners' Guide names Gaius as NuCal's faction agent. No further detail about Gaius — function, status, AI or human — appears in the pack. Source-silent.
- **Darius and the Anomaly**: Darius is NuCal's agricultural AI operating within Dire Marsh. The pack establishes the Anomaly as Dire Marsh's "defining curiosity" and a quarantine as evidence of a threat. No volume in the pack directly states Darius's current status or his relationship to the Anomaly or the Novel Contagion. Source-silent.
- **Novel Contagion specifics**: The Dire Marsh quarantine is mentioned as a "credible threat to colony security," but the pack does not name the contaminant or provide a clinical description. The Cerberus code fragment's `query: contagion = true` branch and the quarantine are co-present in the pack but no volume links them explicitly. Source-silent.
- **Cerberus identity**: The Cerberus code fragment summary describes Cerberus as "previously unidentified." Whether Cerberus is a NuCal AI, a UESC AI, or something else is not stated. Source-silent.
- **Clarissa Song and NuCal**: No volume in the pack names Clarissa Song in a NuCal context. Source-silent on any Song-NuCal relationship.
- **Sekiguchi Genetics**: No volume establishes a NuCal-Sekiguchi relationship. Source-silent.
- **NuCal's current Runner operations**: The Runners' Guide states NuCal is "presently" hiring Runners for intel. No volume names a specific NuCal-contracted Runner or mission. Source-silent.
- **Gus Moraine's current status**: The colonist welcome message was pre-recorded before the Marathon's launch. Moraine speaks in past tense relative to the colonists' arrival. His status in present-day M2026 is not addressed. Source-silent.
