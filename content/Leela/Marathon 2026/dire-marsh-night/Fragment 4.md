In its infancy, cryogenic sleep research faced many ethical hurdles. Survival rates in early trials were low in both human and non-human subjects, and even once lethality decreased, side effects were often extreme. Many participants opted out after their first exposure, and there were few options for 'locking' participants into extended trials. Luckily, public education on the subject, alongside increased participation incentives, improved return rates.
