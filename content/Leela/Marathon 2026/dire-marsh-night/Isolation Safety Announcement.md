# Isolation Safety Announcement

ANALYZING…    

FILE TYPE: Recovered Message

PERIOD: Colony Era

TOPIC: Quarantine Site Safety Update

SUMMARY: A recovered text correspondence and complaint between two Quarantine employees on New Cascadia regarding new isolation and safety procedures.

\===================================================================     

  

Jenn

  

You seeing this? Two weeks ago I was on the same shift with Kaur—same shift we've been on since we got here. Then all of a sudden they get moved to some random Sector D crew for no reason. Haven't heard from him since.

  

They really expect us to just eat up this PR-speak Gideon vomits out, don't they? Not me. Not anymore. Something's going on. Let's talk in person.

  

—Scott

  

[FORWARDED MESSAGE BEGINS…

  

[ATTENTION MARSH QUARANTINE ASSIGNEES

  

PROTECT YOURSELF!

  

New Cascadia is our home, and we must work together to keep it safe. For you, and your working family. New guidelines dictate that ALL WORKERS who have come into contact with anomalous material—or residually impacted materials—must report to the Quarantine SECTOR D for a required prolonged ISOLATION session in a UESC ISOLATION POD.

  

ISOLATE PROPERLY!

  

Isolation sessions may run upwards of several days or take as little as a matter of hours. Remember that failing to comply with isolation protocols places your working companions in danger and exposes you to extreme liability for corporate and personal damages. If you are aware of personnel avoiding proper protocol, report it through your on-site supervisor.

  

WE CARE!

  

Management wants to keep you safe and productive. If you are feeling ill, have experienced feverish symptoms, or know of anyone in your working family experiencing these symptoms, you are legally required to report it to management for assessment. We know best how to accomplish this task, and workers need not concern themselves with interpreting procedures. Focus on your tasks and let management handle the heavy lifting!

  

Personnel will be screened for exposure levels of contaminants during extended isolation, and those with elevated levels will be moved to external sites for safety reassignments.

  

-Gideon…]
