# UESC Drone Manifest

ANALYZING… 

FILE TYPE: Drone Manifest 

PERIOD: Present Day 

TOPIC: Anomalous Cargo 

SUMMARY: UESC Transport Drone manifest annotated by two UESC officials. Document indicates that materials being transported are dangerously contaminated and unstable after significant exposure to the phenomenon known as "the Anomaly." 

\===================================================================

  

SHIPMENT NUMBER 48729 // MANIFEST // VESSEL UESC-T22 // 2894 

  

CARGO: [REDACTED] [REDACTED] [REDACTED]  

  

[023\_SYAR] Shipment contains all remnants collected from the site of Incident 253 (see Incident Report: Contagious Mimesis). All debris was collected within the vicinity of the Anomaly and should be considered severely contaminated. At the site, crew has followed all standard and nonstandard cleanup procedures to the best of ability: Standard Toxic Substance Disposal Protocol, Unknown Substance Containment Procedure, Biological Hazard Removal and Retainment ("Dyer Method"), Radioactivity Risk Mitigation, Contagion and Contamination Risk Assessment and Isolation. Materials collected appear to be inactive. Per Orion, all analysis is paused. Moving forward with containment procedures with plans for incineration.  

  

[015\_ITRE] Please revise this plan. Orders from HQ are for analysis on 253 materials to begin immediately.  

  

[023\_SYAR] Did Orion back this? His initial instruction was risk mitigation. I noted above that all materials appear to be inactive, but this isn't a reliable indicator of stability. Immediate analysis of materials runs a high risk of another destructive incident.  

  

[015\_ITRE] Confirmed by Orion. UESC personnel with adequate hazardous materials training should start assessment in orbital lab units.  

  

[023\_SYAR] Need to push back here. "Hazardous materials training" doesn't cover materials that defy physics. Strongly suggest defaulting to containment.  

  

[015\_ITRE] Denied. No further risk assessment is required or requested. Assessment should begin immediately in orbital lab units.
